Recent & Upcoming Talks

2021

Translation equivalents aren't special in bilingual vocabulary development: A quantitative model
Here we present a novel approach that models translation equivalent knowledge as a function of bilinguals’ own vocabulary size in each language. Our novel insight is that learning a translation equivalent pair can be described as the joint probability of learning each of the words in the pair.
Nov 6, 2021 The 46th Boston University Conference on Language Development
Rachel K.Y. Tsui, Ana Maria Gonzalez-Barrero, Esther Schott, & Krista Byers-Heinlein

Bilingual infants learn cognates more rapidly than non-cognates
Our findings revealed that form-similar cognates are produced more readily in bilingual infant vocabulary development than non-cognate translation equivalents: effects are small at younger ages, and grow over time.
Nov 5, 2021 The 46th Boston University Conference on Language Development
Lori Mitchell, Rachel K.Y. Tsui, & Krista Byers-Heinlein

2020

A multi-lab study of gaze-following development in monolingual and bilingual infants
Using a multi-lab collaborative approach, we investigated whether language experience could affect the development of gaze-following. Independent of monolingual or bilingual status, early gaze-following development is significantly modulated by age-related changes.
Jul 7, 2020 The 2020 Virtual International Congress of Infant Studies
Rachel K.Y. Tsui, Rachel Barr, Alexis Black, Anna Brown, Samantha Durrant, Anja Gampe, Nayeli Gonzalez-Gomez, Mikolaj Hernik, Marianna Jartó, Ágnes Melinda Kovács, Alexandra Laoun-Rubenstein, Casey Lew-Williams, Ulf Liszkowski, Liquan Liu, Claire Noble, Christine Potter, Joscelin Rocha-Hidalgo, Leher Singh, Melanie Soderstrom, Daan van Renswoude, Ingmar Visser, Connor Waddell, Stephanie Wermelinger, & Krista Byers-Heinlein
